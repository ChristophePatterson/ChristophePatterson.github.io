Hello!

I’m Christophe, a PhD student at Durham University. For my PhD I work on Rubyspot damselflies (Hetaerina) and how behavioural interference between species could impact their ability to range shift. I use a combination of landscape genomics and citizen science data. I’m also a director for the rock pool project. I’m hoping to follow this into a career in academia but keeping an open mind along the way.

Previously I was at Exeter (Penryn) for both my BSc in Evolutionary Biology and my two-year Master by Research. At Exeter I was investigating the range expansion of the [St Piran’s hermit crab], Clibanarius erythropus, to the southwest UK using, population size and morphology surveys, population genetics and larval dispersal models. I grew up just outside of Leeds on the boundary of the Yorkshire dales.

You’ve found my github website where I aim to share interesting and fun things I’ve made.

Outside of lockdown, I head out climbing, orienteering, sea swimming, and cycling.

Feel free to reach out by email anytime! I’m also on Twitter where you can find me usually tweeting around life as a PhD student (Christophe_Patt) and the rock pool project, with some occasional grumblings around cycling infrastructure.
